AntiMicroX is a graphical application that lets you use any gamepad to control applications that don't natively support controllers. Map buttons, triggers, and analog sticks to keyboard keys, mouse movements, or scripts — then save those mappings as profiles for specific games or programs.

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install AntiMicroX">
    Download the package for your platform — Flatpak, AppImage, DEB, RPM, or Windows installer — from the [releases page](https://github.com/AntiMicroX/antimicrox/releases/latest).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your gamepad">
    Plug in your gamepad and launch AntiMicroX. Your controller will appear as a tab in the main window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map your buttons">
    Click any button on the controller diagram to open the mapping dialog. Assign keyboard keys, mouse actions, or scripts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save a profile">
    Go to **File → Save Profile** to save your mappings. Load different profiles for different games or applications.
  </Step>
</Steps>
